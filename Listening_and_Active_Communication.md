# Listening and Active Communication

---

## **1. Active Listening**
### Question 1 – What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?


- **Maintain eye contact** – shows attention and respect to the speaker.  
- **Avoid interrupting** – let the speaker complete their thoughts before responding.  
- **Show that you are listening through body language** – nodding, leaning slightly forward, open posture.  
- **Ask clarifying questions when needed** – helps avoid assumptions and confirms understanding.  
- **Paraphrase or summarize what you heard** – repeat their message in your own words to show understanding.  
- **Reflect emotions of the speaker** – acknowledge feelings like *"I understand that you feel frustrated."*  
- **Remove distractions** – put away phone, stop multitasking, stay mentally present.  
- **Give feedback respectfully** – show interest by responding thoughtfully rather than reacting instantly.

---

## **2. Reflective Listening**
### Question 2 – According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?



- **Listen carefully without judging or interrupting.**  
- **Repeat or paraphrase what the other person said** to show you understood.  
- **Reflect the speaker's feelings**, not only their words.
- **Ask questions to confirm meaning**, so there are no misunderstandings.
- **Check back with the speaker** – *"Did I understand you right?"*
- **Focus on the message, tone and body language**, because communication is not only verbal.

---

## **3. Reflection**
### Question 3 – What are the obstacles in your listening process?


- My mind wanders when the topic is long or uninteresting.  
- I sometimes think about my response instead of fully listening.  
- External distractions like phone notifications, background noise.  
- Jumping to conclusions too fast.  
- Multitasking during conversations.

---

### Question 4 – What can you do to improve your listening?


- Practicing mindfulness and staying present during conversations.  
- Keeping my phone away while talking to someone.  
- Paraphrasing to confirm understanding.  
- Asking clarifying questions instead of assuming.  
- Taking notes during meetings or technical discussions.  
- Giving full attention using eye-contact and nodding.

---

## **4. Types of Communication**
### Question 5 – When do you switch to Passive communication style in your daily life?


- I don’t want conflict or disagreement.
- I feel the other person won't value my opinion.
- I lack confidence or feel nervous in group discussions.
- I choose peace over speaking up for myself.

---

### Question 6 – When do you switch to Aggressive communication styles in your daily life?


- I am frustrated or stressed.
- I feel unheard repeatedly.
- There is time pressure and things are not going right.
- I lose patience and speak without thinking.

---

### Question 7 – When do you switch into Passive Aggressive communication style in your daily life?


- I want to express disagreement indirectly.
- I feel upset but don’t want to confront directly.
- I use sarcasm, silent treatment or subtle taunts.
- I expect others to understand my feelings without saying them clearly.

---

### Question 8 – How can you make your communication assertive?


- **Speaking clearly and respectfully without fear.**
- **Balancing tone – not too soft (passive) or too harsh (aggressive).**
- **Setting boundaries politely** – saying *no* when needed.
- **Managing emotions before responding**, instead of reacting instantly.
- **Listening actively before speaking**, making communication two-way.
- **Stating needs directly**, not through hints or sarcasm.
