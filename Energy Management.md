* Before deadlines or exams

* When there’s uncertainty or lack of clarity

* Feeling energized and motivated
* Looking forward to tasks
* Sense of challenge without dread
* Heart feels engaged, not overwhelmed
* Focused and curious

### Question 4: Paraphrase “Sleep Is Your Superpower” video – only points (brief)

* Sleeping less than ~7–8 hours harms overall health and can age the body. (YouTube Summary)
* Sleep before and after learning helps form and retain memories. (YouTube Summary)
* Lack of sleep increases risk of heart attack, weakens immune system, and raises cancer risk. (Glasp)
* Deep sleep is crucial for memory consolidation. (YouTube Summary)
* Good sleep requires regular sleep schedule and a cool room. (Glasp)
---
### Question 5: What are some ideas you can implement to sleep better?
* Maintain a consistent bedtime and wake-up time
* Avoid caffeine and heavy meals before sleep
* Keep bedroom cool and dark
* Avoid screens before bed
* Do calming breathing or meditation
* If unable to sleep, leave bed and return when tired

### Question 6: Paraphrase “Brain Changing Benefits of Exercise” – minimum 5 points

* Improves mood and energy immediately via increased neurotransmitters (dopamine, serotonin, noradrenaline). (Scribd)
* Boosts focus and attention even after a single workout. (Scribd)
* Strengthens memory and learning by increasing hippocampus volume. (Scribd)
* Long-term exercise changes brain structure and enhances cognitive functions. (Coconote)
* Protects against age-related brain decline, like dementia and Alzheimer’s. (Scribd)
---
### Question 7: What are some steps you can take to exercise more?
* Set scheduled exercise times (e.g., mornings)
* Start with small daily goals (e.g., 20–30 mins walking)
* Choose activities you enjoy (dance, swimming, cycling)
* Use habit stacking (e.g., walk after meals)
* Track progress and reward milestones
* Find exercise buddies or groups
